# GLP-1 Compounded Semaglutide Coverage Policy

## FDA and Regulatory Status
Compounded semaglutide (injectable and oral) occupies a legally complex coverage position:
- FDA placed semaglutide on the drug shortage list in 2022, permitting 503A/503B compounding pharmacies to legally compound semaglutide during the shortage period.
- FDA officially removed injectable semaglutide (Ozempic, Wegovy) from the shortage list in February 2024. Compounded semaglutide is no longer FDA-authorized under shortage exemption for most formulations.
- Salt-based compounded semaglutide (e.g. semaglutide acetate, semaglutide sodium) remains prohibited as these are not the same active ingredient as brand semaglutide.

## Commercial Plan Coverage
Most commercial plans do NOT cover compounded semaglutide:
- Compounded drugs are generally excluded from pharmacy benefit formularies.
- If brand semaglutide (Ozempic, Wegovy, Rybelsus) is on formulary, plans typically require the brand rather than compounded alternative.
- No manufacturer copay assistance is available for compounded versions.

Coverage exceptions may apply if:
- Prescriber documents medical necessity for a specific formulation unavailable commercially (e.g., specific concentration, delivery device accommodation).
- Plan has an exception pathway for compounded preparations with documented clinical necessity.

## Medicare Coverage
Medicare Part D does NOT cover compounded drugs unless they meet specific criteria:
- Compounded drug must be produced by a licensed 503B outsourcing facility.
- Must not be essentially a copy of an approved drug.
- In practice, compounded semaglutide is rarely if ever covered under Medicare Part D.

## Care Team Guidance
If a patient is using compounded semaglutide and commercial coverage is being sought:
1. Verify if brand semaglutide (Ozempic for T2D, Wegovy for obesity) is on the plan formulary.
2. Check PA criteria for brand drug — typically BMI ≥30 or ≥27 with comorbidity for Wegovy; A1C ≥7.0 with metformin failure for Ozempic.
3. If brand is on formulary with PA, pursue PA for brand rather than compounded version.
4. Document any clinical reason brand formulation is unsuitable for the patient.
5. Manufacturer copay programs (Novo Nordisk): available for commercially insured patients for brand drugs only; government plans excluded.
