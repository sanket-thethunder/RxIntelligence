# Medicare Part B vs Part D Drug Coverage Policy

## Part B Coverage (Physician-Administered)
Medicare Part B covers drugs that are:
- Administered by a physician or healthcare provider in a clinical setting.
- Injectable biologics administered in office (e.g., dupilumab, mepolizumab, benralizumab when physician-administered).
- Infusion therapies administered in outpatient hospital or infusion center.

Key details:
- 80/20 cost sharing after deductible (patient pays 20% after Part B deductible).
- No copay assistance programs from manufacturers are allowed for Part B drugs.
- Prior authorization may be required by Medicare Advantage plans.

## Part D Coverage (Self-Administered)
Medicare Part D covers self-administered drugs including:
- Oral medications, self-injectable biologics (e.g., dupilumab autoinjector at home).
- Specialty tier drugs subject to plan formulary.
- Compounded drugs rarely covered — must meet 503B outsourcing facility criteria.

Key details:
- Manufacturer copay cards are prohibited for Part D drugs (anti-kickback statute).
- Patient Assistance Programs (PAPs) may be available for low-income Medicare patients.
- Extra Help / Low Income Subsidy (LIS) program available for qualifying patients.
- GLP-1 agonists for obesity indication (Wegovy): NOT covered under most Part D plans as of 2024; Inflation Reduction Act may expand coverage — verify with specific plan.
- GLP-1 agonists for Type 2 Diabetes (Ozempic, Trulicity): typically covered under Part D with PA.

## Prior Authorization Under Medicare Advantage
Medicare Advantage (Part C) plans may impose:
- Step therapy requirements for biologics (BCBS Medicare Advantage may require anti-IL-5 trial before dupilumab).
- PA renewal requirements every 6-12 months.
- Specialty pharmacy distribution requirements.

## Care Team Action Items for Medicare Patients
1. Confirm whether drug is billed under Part B or Part D — this determines PA pathway and cost-sharing.
2. For biologics: check if patient self-injects at home (Part D) or receives in office (Part B).
3. Do NOT offer manufacturer copay assistance to Medicare patients — federal violation.
4. Direct low-income patients to Extra Help program or manufacturer PAP.
5. For Medicare Advantage: obtain plan-specific formulary and PA criteria directly from the plan.
