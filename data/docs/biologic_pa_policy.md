# Biologic Therapy Prior Authorization Policy

## Dupilumab (Dupixent) — Moderate-to-Severe Asthma

### Coverage Criteria (Commercial Plans)
Prior authorization is required for dupilumab for asthma indication.

Approval criteria:
- Diagnosis of moderate-to-severe persistent asthma confirmed by pulmonologist or allergist.
- FEV1 less than 80% predicted on spirometry, documented within 12 months.
- Blood eosinophil count of 150 cells/uL or greater OR FeNO 25 ppb or greater (Type 2 / eosinophilic phenotype).
- Failure, intolerance, or contraindication to at least one preferred biologic (mepolizumab, benralizumab, or tezepelumab) if required by plan step therapy policy.
- Current treatment with high-dose inhaled corticosteroid (ICS) plus long-acting beta-agonist (LABA), documented for at least 3 months.
- Two or more asthma exacerbations in the prior 12 months requiring oral corticosteroid, or one severe exacerbation requiring hospitalization or ER visit.

### Step Therapy Requirements — BCBS Commercial PPO
BCBS Commercial PPO policies typically require step therapy through at least one FDA-approved anti-IL-5 or anti-IL-5R biologic before approving dupilumab UNLESS:
- Patient has documented failure of anti-IL-5 therapy (mepolizumab or benralizumab) with discontinuation due to adverse events (injection site reactions qualify), OR
- Prescriber attests that the IL-4/IL-13 dual pathway (dupilumab mechanism) is clinically preferred based on comorbid atopic dermatitis, eosinophilic esophagitis, or nasal polyposis, OR
- Blood eosinophils are below 150 cells/uL (ruling out IL-5 pathway as primary driver).

### Medical Necessity Exception — IL-4/IL-13 Pathway Argument
When requesting direct dupilumab approval after failed anti-IL-5 therapy, the care team should include:
1. Documentation of failed biologic trial (drug name, doses administered, discontinuation reason).
2. Comorbid Type 2 inflammatory conditions (atopic dermatitis, eosinophilic esophagitis, allergic rhinitis, nasal polyps).
3. Peer-reviewed clinical rationale: dupilumab's dual IL-4/IL-13 blockade addresses Th2 inflammation broadly, whereas anti-IL-5 agents target eosinophilia specifically. NEJM 2018 Liberty Asthma QUEST trial data supports dupilumab in patients with eosinophil counts as low as 150 cells/uL.
4. Prescriber attestation from pulmonologist or allergist with NPI.

### Medicare Coverage
Dupilumab for asthma is covered under Medicare Part B (physician-administered) or Part D (self-administered). Government-funded plans are NOT eligible for manufacturer copay assistance (Dupixent MyWay program). Patient assistance programs may be available for qualifying low-income patients.

## Mepolizumab (Nucala) — Eosinophilic Asthma

### Coverage Criteria
- Blood eosinophils 150 cells/uL or greater at initiation, 300 cells/uL or greater preferred.
- Severe eosinophilic asthma with 2+ exacerbations in prior year.
- Ongoing high-dose ICS/LABA therapy.
- BCBS Commercial PPO typically lists as preferred biologic before dupilumab for eosinophilic phenotype.

### Step Therapy Context
Mepolizumab is often required as step therapy BEFORE dupilumab on BCBS commercial formularies unless clinical factors support IL-4/IL-13 pathway preference. Failure of mepolizumab (including adverse event discontinuation) satisfies step therapy for dupilumab approval.

## Benralizumab (Fasenra) — Eosinophilic Asthma

### Coverage Criteria
- Blood eosinophils 300 cells/uL or greater preferred; 150 cells/uL minimum.
- Severe persistent asthma.
- Injection site reactions are a documented reason for discontinuation and satisfy step therapy failure criteria.

### Step Therapy Context
Documented failure/discontinuation of benralizumab due to injection site reactions satisfies step therapy requirement for subsequent biologic approval including dupilumab under most BCBS commercial policies.

## Documentation Checklist for PA Submission
- Pulmonologist/allergist notes with NPI
- Spirometry report (FEV1 % predicted)
- FeNO measurement if available
- Blood eosinophil count (CBC with differential)
- Oral corticosteroid burst history (dates, doses)
- ER/hospitalization history for asthma
- Prior biologic trial documentation (drug, dose, dates, reason for discontinuation)
- Current medication list showing ICS/LABA therapy
- Letter of medical necessity from prescriber
